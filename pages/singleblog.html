High blood pressure, also known as hypertension, affects millions worldwide. It is a condition that increases the risk of various health problems such as strokes, heart attacks, and more. While stress is one well-known cause of hypertension, a poor diet, smoking, and lack of exercise, are other factors that can contribute to high blood pressure. Health Tip: Research shows that people who don't get enough quality sleep are at risk of developing hypertension. This is because sleep regulates the body's stress hormones, which affect blood pressure. If you're not getting enough restful sleep, each night it could be contributing to your high blood pressure.
